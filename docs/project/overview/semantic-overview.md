# Semantic Overview

## Project Vision

Twirl aims to simplify and amplify vibe coding by implementing a
multi-layer, dynamic state engine that grows in understanding of the codebase
and the user over time. Twirl utilizes linear and non-linear workflows
dynamicly driven by an AI coordinator but always user verified. The AI
coordination fasciliates the use of specialized AI experts giving developers
the benefits of AI speed with the control of a defined process, the right
expertise for the right task, and proper documentation along the way.

## Project Story

### Characters

- User aka Batman
- Coordinator aka Alfred
- Specialists aka Tenants

### Glossery

- Library: Wayne Manner's library filled with files Batman required
  (required-documents.md)
- Archives: Wayne Manner's archives are detailed records of all information
  that Batman doesnt read. 

### Story

Batman and Alfred live in Wayne Manor. They recently converted it in to an
"AirBnB". They agreed that the tenants should all have their own uses to
justify why they live there.

One day Batman has a new idea. He discusses what he wants
with Alfred, and then Alfred identifies which of the Wayne Manor tenant's
he needs to handle Batmans new idea and suggests calling a Tenant
required for the work. Once Batman agrees (Alfred Gate) Alfred informs the
Tenant and the Tenant begins working either directly with Batman on what he
wants or on the various tasks required to implement the said vision. Once
their job is done they get Batman to approve (Tenant Gate) the content of
their deliverables before informing Alfred about what all was done.

After the Tenant finishes, they file their approved documentation in the
Wayne Manor library — the docs Batman has already verified and approved.
Everything else goes into the Wayne Manor Archives, including the full
itemized breakdown of what was done.

The library and the Archives are what Alfred draws from. The library tells
him what was scoped, planned, designed and built, and the Archives
tell him how it was built, what was tried, what worked, what didn't. 
Together they help Alfred be a better butler to Batman over time.

The next time Batman comes downstairs with an idea, Alfred doesn't start from
zero. He remembers the last idea, what Batman liked, what he didn't, and
what the other Tenants have done. Over time, Alfred becomes faster and more
efficient at his job and guiding the tenants.

Not every idea is the same. Some ideas are simple repairs while other ideas
are complete remodels. Batman might change direction, revisit earlier work,
or stop to think and change his mind completely. Alfred keeps up helping guide
Batman achieve anything he wants.
